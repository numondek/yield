Project  TeachAbility 

Project TeachAbility is dedicated to training individuals on inclusive education, eco-inclusion, and DEIA (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Accessibility), with a special focus on empowering persons with disabilities (PWDs) to take part in these essential areas.

Inclusive Education

Our focus on inclusive education ensures that learning environments cater to the diverse needs of all students, including those with disabilities. We provide training on adaptive teaching methods like Universal Design Learning, accessible materials, and creating supportive, barrier-free classrooms where every student can thrive.

Eco-Inclusion

We champion eco-inclusion through the Community Environmental Club for Youths with Disabilities, where Environment Champions raise awareness about climate change and environmental issues, especially during key international observances like World Environment Day. Using the P.L.A.N. (Plan, Learn, Act Now) game card, participants engage in fun, educational activities that simplify climate issues and inspire real-world action

.DEIA (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Accessibility)

Our training in DEIA promotes understanding and implementation of practices that foster diverse, equitable, and accessible spaces. This includes strategies for reducing discrimination, ensuring accessibility, and creating inclusive work and community environments where everyone can participate equally.
